    Feasibility Study

    Your land parcel is waiting to host the next best project in your city. Should the project be an office project, retail mall or high street, a housing project, a co-living asset or even a warehouse? CRE Matrix RAW assesses the viability of a project prior to the formal planning process using data as a core along with our in-depth field research to clearly understand where the market is moving towards.     Highest & Best Use Study

    We study the local real estate market, including supply and demand factors, to determine the most profitable use for the property. Our approach start from analysis of the subject site - evaluating the physical characteristics of the property, including size, location, zoning, and topography, to determine its suitability for different uses. We then move on to financial analysis to gauge the financial performance of different uses, including estimated revenue, expenses, and profitability. Our Legal and Regulatory Analysis covers any legal or regulatory constraints that may affect the property, including zoning laws, environmental regulations, and historical preservation restrictions.

    We have developed internal models that crunch tonnes of data so that the CRE RAW HBU produces the highest value for a property based on the concept of maximum productivity by determining the most optimum type of the property that can be developed and that would result in the highest market value of the property.

    TEV Study

    RAW TEV study measure market risk, regulatory risk and financial risk of a project. Techno Economic Viability (TEV) study of a project consists of evaluation of a project from a technical and financial standpoint, with relevant data about its technological feasibility and economic viability, into one or a few criteria on the basis of which the project is recommended for selection, modification or rejection.

    Lenders use CRE RAW Techno-Economic Viability (TEV) Study to take decide the acceptability of the degree of risk involved in a project. We take into account technological risk, market risk, regulatory risk, financial risk, etc. to arrive at a risk score for the project under analysis.
